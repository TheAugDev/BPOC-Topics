Interviews & Interrogations

Denton Police Training Academy

1. Definitions & Legal Framework

Interview

A non-accusatory conversation with a cooperative individual to gather information about a crime or incident.

Interrogation

An accusatory conversation with a suspect designed to elicit incriminating responses. Requires Miranda warnings when in custody.

Miranda Requirements

Required when: Suspect is in custody AND being interrogated

The Warnings:

  • You have the right to remain silent
  • Anything you say can be used against you in court
  • You have the right to an attorney
  • If you cannot afford an attorney, one will be appointed for you

2. Interview Techniques

Preparation
  • Review all available evidence and reports
  • Prepare questions in advance
  • Choose appropriate location and time
  • Ensure recording equipment is working
Building Rapport
  • Use active listening techniques
  • Show empathy and understanding
  • Find common ground
  • Maintain professional demeanor
Question Types

Open-ended: Allow for detailed responses (Who, What, When, Where, How)

Closed-ended: Require specific yes/no or short answers

Leading: Generally avoided as they suggest the answer

Active Listening
  • Pay attention to verbal and non-verbal cues
  • Paraphrase and clarify responses
  • Allow for pauses and silence
  • Take detailed notes

3. Interrogation Methods

Reid Technique

A widely used interrogation method with nine steps:

  1. Direct positive confrontation
  2. Theme development
  3. Handling denials
  4. Overcoming objections
  5. Procurement of suspect's attention
  6. Handling suspect's passive mood
  7. Presenting alternative questions
  8. Having suspect detail events
  9. Converting oral confession to written
PEACE Model

An alternative approach focused on information gathering:

  • Preparation and Planning
  • Engage and Explain
  • Account, Clarify, Challenge
  • Closure
  • Evaluation
Cognitive Interview

Technique designed to enhance memory recall:

  • Reconstruct the circumstances
  • Report everything
  • Recall events in different orders
  • Change perspectives

4. Legal Considerations

Voluntary Statements

Must be given freely without coercion, threats, or promises of leniency.

Prohibited Tactics

Physical force, threats, prolonged interrogation, deprivation of basic needs.

Custody vs. Non-Custody

Custody: Person reasonably believes they are not free to leave. Miranda required for interrogation.

Non-Custody: Person is free to leave. Miranda not required.

Juvenile Considerations
  • Must understand rights in age-appropriate language
  • Parent/guardian notification often required
  • Time limits on interrogation
  • Special protections against coercion

5. Documentation & Best Practices

Recording Recommendations

  • Record entire interview/interrogation when possible
  • Use both audio and video when available
  • Document any technical issues
  • Obtain written statements when appropriate
Report Writing
  • Document time, date, location, and participants
  • Record Miranda warnings if given
  • Summarize key statements and admissions
  • Note any unusual circumstances
  • Include witness information if present
