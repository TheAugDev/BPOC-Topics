Firearms Training Guide

Denton Police Training Academy

1. Universal Firearms Safety Rules

1

Treat Every Firearm as if it is Loaded

Never assume a firearm is unloaded. Always check the chamber and magazine.

2

Never Point the Muzzle at Anything You Don't Want to Destroy

Control muzzle direction at all times. Be aware of what is beyond your target.

3

Keep Your Finger Off the Trigger

Until you are ready to shoot. Keep your finger along the frame or trigger guard.

4

Be Sure of Your Target and What's Beyond

Identify your target and be aware of what is behind it before firing.

2. Marksmanship Fundamentals

Stance

Isosceles Stance: Square to the target, feet shoulder-width apart, both arms extended equally.

Weaver Stance: Bladed to the target, strong-side foot back, strong arm extended, support arm bent and pulling.

Modified Weaver: Combination of both, allowing for natural body mechanics.

Grip

High on the backstrap, consistent pressure from both hands. Support hand fills the void, thumbs forward technique recommended for semi-automatics.

Sight Alignment

Front sight post centered in the rear sight notch with equal amounts of light on both sides. Top of front sight level with top of rear sight.

Sight Picture

Proper sight alignment placed on the target. Focus on the front sight, not the target or rear sight.

Trigger Control

Straight back pressure with the pad of the index finger. Smooth, consistent squeeze without disturbing sight alignment.

Follow Through

Maintain sight picture and grip through the shot. Call your shots based on where the sights were when the gun fired.

3. Weapon Handling & Manipulations

Loading

Insert loaded magazine, rack the slide to chamber a round. Ensure the weapon is pointed in a safe direction throughout the process.

Unloading

Remove magazine first, then rack the slide to eject chambered round. Visually and physically inspect the chamber.

Immediate Action (Type 1 Malfunction)

TAP-RACK-ASSESS: Tap the magazine to ensure it's seated, rack the slide to clear any malfunction, assess the situation.

Remedial Action (Type 2 Malfunction)

LOCK-RIP-RACK-ASSESS: Lock the slide back, rip out the magazine, rack the slide multiple times, assess and reload if necessary.

Tactical Reload

Reload when you have time and cover, retaining the partially used magazine for later use.

Emergency Reload

Reload when the weapon is empty during a threat situation. Speed is priority, drop the empty magazine.

4. Tactical Considerations

Use of Cover vs. Concealment

Cover: Protection from enemy fire (concrete walls, engine blocks, large trees).

Concealment: Hides you from view but offers no ballistic protection (bushes, car doors, drywall).

Shooting Positions

Standing: Fastest to assume, least stable. Kneeling: More stable than standing, good compromise. Prone: Most stable, longest to assume.

Low Light Shooting

Use of weapon-mounted lights or handheld lights. Master the light, don't let it master you. Identify target before shooting.

Multiple Target Engagement

Engage closest threat first, or most dangerous threat. Use proper target transitions without over-swinging.

5. TCOLE Qualification Standards

Weapon Type Distance Rounds Qualifying Score
Handgun 3, 7, 15, 25 yards 50 70%
Shotgun 7, 15, 25 yards 25 70%
Rifle 50, 100 yards 40 70%

Important Notes:

  • Officers must qualify annually with their duty weapon
  • Qualification must be conducted by certified instructors
  • Failure to qualify results in duty restriction until requalification
  • Some departments may have higher standards than TCOLE minimums
